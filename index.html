CASE 01 · GEOSPATIAL ML

Comparing K-means and Random Forest for multi-year land-cover classification

A four-year, Sentinel-2-based study of how unsupervised vs. supervised approaches differ when the goal isn't just accuracy — but temporal consistency and computational cost.

Python scikit-learn Sentinel-2 QGIS Google Colab Remote sensing
Problem
How do unsupervised and supervised classifiers compare for monitoring multi-year land-cover change — not just on accuracy, but on temporal stability and compute cost?
Method
Sentinel-2 imagery (2020–2024) over a fixed AOI; preprocessing in Python; K-means (unsupervised) vs. Random Forest (supervised) trained on labelled samples; per-year confusion matrices and inter-year change detection.
Tools
Python · scikit-learn · rasterio · NumPy · QGIS · Google Colab. Carbon footprint estimated via runtime × compute energy intensity.
Results
Random Forest delivered higher per-year accuracy; K-means produced more temporally consistent outputs and a markedly lower compute footprint — a real trade-off for repeated monitoring tasks.
CASE 02 · DATA STORYTELLING

An animated 30-year journey through per-capita CO₂ emissions

Pulling World Bank time-series and turning it into a visual narrative — designed to make a complex, three-decade dataset legible in under a minute.

R ggplot2 gganimate wbstats dplyr Storytelling
Problem
Per-capita emissions diverge wildly between countries and decades. How can 30 years of data across hundreds of countries be communicated to a non-specialist audience in seconds?
Method
Pulled World Bank indicators via the wbstats API; cleaned and reshaped in dplyr; year-by-year frames rendered with ggplot2 and stitched into a smooth animation with gganimate.
Tools
R · ggplot2 · gganimate · wbstats · dplyr · tidyr. Output as MP4 + GIF for portability.
Results
A 30-second animation that makes the global emissions story visceral — used as a template for further indicator stories (energy intensity, forest cover, GDP per capita).
